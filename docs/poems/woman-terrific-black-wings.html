Woman (Terrific Black Wings)

Woman,


    I desire;


To guard the spark of life

    beneath terrific black wings,

    to inspire dread

    in twisted hearts that

    openly conspire to consume

    soft flesh

    of fresh life.


And so I need

    you to guide me back,

    away from ravine edge,

    to the wide level plain

    of fertile faith:


Faith in love,

    faith in joy,

    faith in morning spent

        gathering and breaking wood

        for our hearth;

    faith that night can come as friend

        of deep rest

        and just recompense.


I wonder, now,

    while wind blows through

    bare brittle trees:

what will become of

    the names we have

    imagined for our young?

Will leaves take hold on bare branches?

What will be borne

    of the struggle we have shouldered

    and our nearly-clotted blood?

I wonder about seasons

    turning over and over upon earth:

    will we let them lay down

        fresh, dark soil upon

        our souls?


I pine,

    in the blue moments before full wakefulness,

        for release from needle

        sin and weighty despair,

    to join hot palms

    and dive into rich,

        ecstatic goodness,

        unchanging and ever-surprising,

    to be consumed

        and rise

        with golden wind,

    to soar

        slow and steady

        into a morn turning from

        suffocation toward light,

    to roam with strength

        into open air.
